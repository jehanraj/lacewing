Bright House Dish Soap Sucks

By Cody Burawski

Bright House Dish Soap is some kind of vague imaginary dish soap brand from Dollar General. The packaging doesn’t have much info on it. It was made in Illinois and it’s got a little American flag on it because it is very proud of this. It claims to have an “advanced formula with powerful degreasers.”


More like Bright House Dish Nope. Photo by Cody Burawski.

Bright House should not feel proud. Because this dish soap is total bullshit.

It looks like normal dish soap. It’s blue and foams up as though it is actually doing something, but don’t let it fool you: this soap can not clean. You’re better off pouring a rootbeer float on your dishes. You’re better off telling the food residue on your dirty dishes that it’s too old to still be living in your house and needs to go somewhere else and make something of itself. You’re better off throwing your dishes in the ocean and combing the beach the next morning hoping they’ve chosen to return to you. These actions will get you further than using Bright House Dish Soap. It is basically water.

I haven’t had any other problems with generic stuff at Dollar General. Their generic boxed macaroni and cheese seems like it’s actually the exact same substance in Kraft’s in a different package, but it costs about forty cents. That stuff is sweet.
